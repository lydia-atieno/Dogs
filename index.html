DOG BREEDS

Dog Facts

  • Their sense of smell is atleast 40x better than ours.
  • Some dogs are incredible swimmers.
  • Dogs have 18 muscles controlling their ears.
  • Dogs are about as intelligent as a two year old.
  • Dog noses are wet to help absorb scent chemicals.
  • Dogs can be trained to detect cancer and other diseases in humans through smelling someone's breath.
  • Dogs dream when they sleep.
  • Dogs can be right-pawed or left-pawed.
  • Smaller dogs live longer.

    • The German Shepherd

    As the name implies, this breed originated from Germany. The coat is usually black and tan but can have other color combinations.

  • Best trained from an early age, they are intelligent and obedient animals that easily learn.
  • They are faithful to their masters and always ready to protect their masters.
  • They don't bark at people aimlessly and only does so under aggression.
  • Having an independent character and being very energetic the breed is not good indoors, always needing space to channel their excess energy.
  • They are used as guard dogs, for police work, guide for the blind search and rescue services, and in the millitary.
  • The GSD also excels in tracking and sports.
  • It has a fine nose useful for sniffing out intruders, drugs, gas leaks and underground mines.
  • They have a lifespan of 9 to 13 years.

    • The Rottweiler

    This breed also has its origin in Germany. The color is predominantly black and sometimes with brownish patches. It is muscular and massive with a powerful body.

  • A well trained rottweiler is very cooperative.
  • It has a tendency for unconditional devotion to the family and especially one person.
  • The breed needs alot of leadership and socialization.
  • Rottweiler thrives on lots of exercises. Daily walks, jogs or running along a bicycle makes them very happy.
  • They have a lifespan of 8 to 10 years.

    • Neapolitan Mastiff

    Neopolitan Mastiff has its origin in Italy. Color ranges from black, brown, grey or blue-grey.

  • It is a very serious, massive and powerful dog
  • The breed can be intimidating but is actually affectionate, calm and peaceful.
  • It is loving especially with children, enjoying family and friends but reserved with strangers.
  • They tend to dominate other dogs and do not socialize with other animals if not brought up together as a puppy.
  • It only barks when necessary.
  • Drooling is a common characteristic.
  • The dog needs a predominant owner who must control them properly.
  • It has an average lifespan of 8 to 10 years.

    • Rhodesian Ridgeback

    Rhodesian Ridgeback originated from Zimbabwe where it was used for hunting lions. Its color ranges from white to red.

  • The dog is very intelligent and possesses very high stamina which should be nurtured through training and socializing from an early age for them to get along with other pets.
  • A well trained rhodesian ridgeback is gentle and very obedient, loyal independent and aloof to strangers, but sometimes can be very rough with children.
  • If bored they can be very destructive displaying a wide range of behavioral problems.
  • Not everyone can own this dog because they can be very stubborn if they sense that they are stronger than their master.
  • They are used for hunting, guarding , retrieving, taking care of children and herding animals.
  • They have ana average span of 10 to 12 years.

    • Labrador Retriever

    It originated from Labrador in England. The smooth coat is black, yellow or chocolate in color.

  • The breed loves to play a lot especially in water.
  • They are loyal, loving and patient hence make very excellent family dogs.
  • Being very intelligent and easy learning the breed can be trained in a wide range of activities such as tracking, detecting weapon, hunting, retrieving, guiding the blind, search and rescue and other duties.
  • Its lifespan is somewhere between 10 and 14 years.

    • The English Springer Spaniel

    It originated from England. The coat color is predominantly black and white but their are other variations such as dark red and white.

  • Without clear rules, the dog can become frustrated and destructive, where it barks a lot especially if left alone.
  • The dog habitually tests the leadership position and does not respond well to harsh discipline. Therefore the owner must consistently exercise a lot of calm authority and guidance.
  • The breed easily adapts well to town life and does well indoors if provided with enough exercise.
  • They are trained for hunting, tacking, detecting explosive and drugs, search and performing tricks.
  • It has an average lifespan of 12 to 15 years.

    • Doberman Pinscher

    It originated from Germany. The color varies from black, red, blue and fawn.

  • They are keen and aggressive animals with lots of energy and strength.
  • They like the company of people but not being isolated in the backyard.
  • They are adaptable, highly skilled and versatile.
  • All family members need to be firm, consistent and authoritative over the dog, as they can become stubborn if they are allowed to have their own way.
  • Their sensitivity to cold makes them unsuitable for outdoor activities.
  • Their excellent sense of smell makes them ideal for tracking, search and rescue, guarding, watching, poliIt ce and military work.
  • They can live upto 10 to 13 years.

    • Basenji

    Originated from Zaire. They are smooth athletic dogs with pure shiny black, copper, red or a combination of those coat colors.

  • They are considered to be the cleanest. They wash themselves like cats.
  • They do not have dog smell hence very little grooming is required.
  • The dog is alert, strong, loving and curious.
  • They don't bark but make some other noises like screaming or howling depending on the mood.
  • They dislike hot weather and cold temperatures.
  • The dog frequently and cleverly gets its way by being stubborn as opposed to being charismatic.
  • It is mainly used for hunting.
  • It is recommended to persons with lots of dog experience.
  • Although they are active indoors, they do well when there are two or three more of their kind.
  • It has an average lifespan of 10 to 12 years.

    • Chihuahua

    It originated from Mexico where it has been used as companions. Color varies from white, black, silver, blue, tan and fawn.

  • It is the smallest of all the dog breeds.
  • Preferring warm weather and enough space for exercise, the dog is intelligent, quick to learn and responds well to training.
  • Because humans tend to babysit them, they develop a habit of being attached to theThe owner.
  • The dog is not recommended for children if training is not done properly.
  • The dog has an average lifespan of 12 to 14 years.

    • Pomeranian

    The breed has its origin in Germany. The interest by Queen Victoria for this breed led to its popularity. Common colors are black, white, orange, grey and brown.

  • They have long coats which should be brushed frequently.
  • The dog is intelligent, learns easily and loyal.
  • It is very noisy.
  • Eating can be a problem at times beacuse of their picky nature.
  • They are liked bacause of their proud and lively nature.
  • They get along well with other dogs and domestic animals if properly socialized and guided with clear rules and limits.
  • They are very good at learning tricks.
  • They make good watchdogs and indoor companions for the elderly.
  • The life expectancy of a pomeranian is 12 to 16 years.

    • Maltese

    This breed originated from Malta where they got their name. They either have long white or ivory colored silky hair.

  • Being highly intelligent dogs they learn tricks quickly and are always ready to sound an alarm in case of suspicion.
  • They love to play outdoors and go easily with other domestic animals.
  • They can live for upto around 15 years.

    • Dog care tips.

      Veterinarian visits.
      Vaccinations.
      Healthy food.
      Training and socializing.
